About this program

Equipping parents for the digital age

Children today navigate a world their parents never grew up in — smartphones, social media, online predators and instant access to harmful content. Parenting & Sex-Educating the Tech Age Child (PSETAC) is a free monthly program that bridges that gap.

Anchored by Dr. John S. Balogun, founder and convener of the CASRHA Foundation, each session addresses a specific aspect of raising sexually informed children in a digitally connected world — from setting boundaries around screen time to recognising online grooming and having age-appropriate conversations about the body.

  • Monthly interactive sessions via Google Meet
  • Practical, age-appropriate strategies for every parent
  • 100% free — open to all parents, guardians and mentors
